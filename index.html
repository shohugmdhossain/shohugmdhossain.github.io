AI for Earth & Planetary Discovery

I explore how artificial intelligence and satellite observations can unlock new insights about the surfaces of Earth and Mars. My research bridges machine learning, geospatial analytics, and planetary geomorphology to model landscapes and environmental change.

At the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, I develop GeoAI frameworks for large-scale planetary image interpretation using high-performance GPUs. My goal is to build interpretable, scalable systems that connect Earth observation with planetary exploration — revealing shared patterns in surface processes across worlds.

My academic journey combines data science, engineering, and geoscience. I’m motivated by the belief that intelligent models can extend human perception — transforming how we see, measure, and understand our planet and its analogs beyond Earth.
