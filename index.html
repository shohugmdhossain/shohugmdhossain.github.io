AI for Earth & Planetary Discovery

My research explores how artificial intelligence and satellite observations can uncover new insights about planetary and terrestrial surfaces. Through GeoAI and remote sensing, I analyze how geomorphic and environmental processes evolve across Earth and Mars.

At the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, I develop scalable deep learning workflows for detecting landforms, spectral unmixing, and planetary image interpretation using HPC infrastructure. These efforts support the creation of reproducible datasets for cross-planet geomorphology.

My work bridges computer science, geoscience, and data engineering, with a goal of translating complex imagery into interpretable models of landscape evolution. From urban terrain mapping to Martian surface reconstruction, I seek to extend the boundaries of environmental and planetary understanding through machine intelligence.
