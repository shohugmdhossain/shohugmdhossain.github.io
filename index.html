Md Shohug Hossain, Ph.D. Researcher

I explore how artificial intelligence and satellite data together can transform our understanding of the planet — and of worlds beyond it. My research brings together GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis to study geomorphology and environmental dynamics across Earth and Mars.

At the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, my work focuses on large-scale planetary image interpretation and AI-driven landform classification using high-performance computing environments. My broader interest is building interpretable machine-learning tools that bridge planetary exploration, climate science, and data analytics.

Previously trained in engineering and data science, I now combine that computational foundation with geoscience to pursue questions of planetary evolution, habitability, and environmental change. I am passionate about using data-driven discovery to map the unseen — from urban systems to Martian surfaces.
