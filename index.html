EARTH & PLANETARY SCIENCE • GEOAI

Turning satellite data into actionable insights for Earth and planetary science

I’m a Ph.D. researcher specializing in remote sensing, satellite imagery, and geospatial AI. My work combines machine learning and high-performance computing to study landscapes on Earth and Mars. I focus on translating complex datasets into insights that drive climate action, urban planning, and planetary science.

Currently Working On:

• Mars Mounds Detection and Classification — Developing Faster R-CNN models for mapping mound-like geomorphology in CTX imagery.

• Planetary Surface Evolution Modeling — Integrating multispectral datasets to analyze temporal surface dynamics across planetary environments.

Research Interests

  • Planetary geomorphology and landscape evolution on Mars and Earth.
  • Automated crater, mound, and surface feature detection using deep learning and computer vision.
  • GeoAI for Earth observation, hazard mapping, and climate analytics.
  • Integrating multispectral and radar data for planetary surface interpretation.

Recent Updates

  • Presented “Mars Mounds Detection with Deep Learning” at UL-L Geoscience Seminar (Oct 2025).
  • Preparing a manuscript on automated mound morphology classification using CNN-based feature extraction.
  • Building a new Martian surface benchmark dataset combining CTX and HiRISE imagery.

Next Goals

  • Publish Mounds Detection Dataset v2 with public COCO annotations.
  • Collaborate on cross-planet geomorphology modeling using Earth analogs.
  • Expand GeoAI to detect ancient fluvial/volcanic features in high-res imagery.
