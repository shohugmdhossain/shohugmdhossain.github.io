ABOUT ME

Ph.D. Researcher in GeoAI, Remote Sensing & Planetary Science

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, working at the intersection of Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), Remote Sensing, and Earth & Planetary Science. My research integrates high-resolution satellite imagery, computer vision, and machine learning to understand surface processes on Earth and Mars.

I am passionate about leveraging AI and large-scale satellite data to map environmental changes, analyze urban infrastructure, and reveal geomorphic evolution on planetary surfaces. My recent work involves Faster R-CNN and YOLO-based object detection applied to Martian CTX imagery and urban sidewalk inventories, using GPU-powered HPC clusters for training and evaluation.

Research Interests

  • Planetary geomorphology and Mars surface mapping
  • Multispectral and hyperspectral image analysis
  • Urban analytics and infrastructure mapping using GeoAI
  • Physics-informed neural networks and interpretability

Beyond Research

Outside of research, I enjoy building open-source tools, mentoring students in data analysis and visualization, and contributing to academic collaborations across geoscience and computer vision. I’m deeply motivated by the idea that Earth observation and planetary exploration share a common goal — understanding our dynamic world and beyond.
