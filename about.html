About Me

Md Shohug Hossain is a Ph.D. researcher specializing in GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary science.

Ph.D. Researcher, Department of Earth and Energy Sciences · University of Louisiana at Lafayette, USA

My research bridges artificial intelligence with Earth and planetary data analysis. I develop deep-learning and physics-informed frameworks to interpret satellite imagery, uncovering geomorphic and environmental processes on Earth and Mars. I focus on creating transparent, reproducible GeoAI methods that advance planetary surface interpretation, hazard mapping, and data-driven environmental science.

My background combines computer science, data engineering, and geospatial analysis. I am motivated by interdisciplinary research that connects technology, geoscience, and scientific communication.

Research Interests

  • Planetary geomorphology and surface-process modeling
  • Deep learning for planetary and terrestrial feature detection
  • Multisensor data fusion: radar, optical, and hyperspectral integration
  • AI-driven Earth observation and climate resilience analysis

Technical Skills

  • Programming & AI: Python, PyTorch, Detectron2, YOLOv8, Scikit-learn
  • Geospatial Tools: ArcGIS, QGIS, Google Earth Engine, PostGIS
  • Data & Visualization: NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Remote Sensing: CRISM, Sentinel, Landsat, multispectral & hyperspectral imagery
  • Systems: Linux, Slurm, HPC computing
  • Web Development: HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Academic Journey

  • Ph.D. in Earth & Energy Sciences — University of Louisiana at Lafayette
    Focus: Planetary geomorphology, GeoAI, and remote sensing.
  • M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering — University of Calgary
    Focus: Intelligent automation and geospatial analytics.
  • B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering — North South University
    Focus: Software engineering and data science foundations.

Beyond Research

Beyond academic research, I am passionate about leadership, outreach, and science communication. At the University of Calgary, I served as President of the ECE Graduate Group and Vice-President of the Schulich Graduate Engineering Consortium, fostering collaboration and mentorship. I enjoy academic writing, visual storytelling, and web-based science communication that bridges technology and public understanding.
