About Md Shohug Hossain

Md Shohug Hossain is a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette specializing in GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary science. His research explores the intersection of machine learning and geoscience to analyze geomorphological evolution on Earth and Mars.

He is passionate about developing deep learning systems that transform planetary imagery into scientific knowledge—bridging computation, observation, and environmental understanding.

Academic & Research Journey

Ph.D. — University of Louisiana at Lafayette (2025–Present)

Doctoral research in GeoAI and planetary geomorphology using deep learning and high-resolution satellite data.

M.Sc. — University of Calgary (2023)

Master’s research focused on machine learning and satellite image analysis for Earth observation and computer vision.

B.Sc. — North South University (2019)

Computer Science & Engineering major with specialization in data analysis and image processing; merit scholarship recipient.

Areas of Expertise

GeoAI & Deep Learning

Developing convolutional and transformer-based neural networks for planetary surface mapping and geoscientific pattern detection.

Remote Sensing & GIS

Processing and interpreting multispectral satellite data to study terrain features, morphology, and temporal change detection.

HPC & Cloud Computing

Leveraging GPU clusters and cloud pipelines for scalable Earth and planetary image analytics.

Beyond Research

Outside academia, Shohug enjoys combining art, science, and exploration. His interests include astrophotography, stargazing, and discovering geological landscapes that mirror planetary forms. He also mentors junior researchers and promotes scientific outreach to inspire curiosity about space, Earth systems, and AI for sustainability.
