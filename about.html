About Me

Md Shohug Hossain is a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette specializing in GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary science.

Ph.D. Researcher, Department of Earth and Energy Sciences · University of Louisiana at Lafayette, USA

My academic work focuses on developing intelligent systems that integrate artificial intelligence with geospatial data to interpret planetary and terrestrial environments. By combining deep learning with physical models, I explore how landforms evolve on Mars and how Earth’s surface processes can be understood through remote sensing and data analytics.

Previously, I studied intelligent automation and geospatial analytics during my M.Sc. at the University of Calgary and earned a B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering from North South University. My interdisciplinary background allows me to approach geoscientific questions through the lens of computation, data science, and systems engineering.

My long-term vision is to advance trustworthy, physics-informed GeoAI frameworks that can enhance planetary exploration, improve climate resilience studies, and foster open, reproducible science.

Research Interests

My research explores the intersection of artificial intelligence, geospatial science, and planetary geomorphology — connecting Earth observation with planetary exploration to better understand how landscapes evolve across diverse environments.

  • Planetary geomorphology and automated surface-process modeling
  • AI-driven landform detection and classification across Mars and Earth
  • Fusion of radar, optical, and hyperspectral datasets for terrain analysis
  • Physics-informed and interpretable GeoAI for planetary-scale understanding
  • Trustworthy and open-source frameworks for reproducible geospatial analytics
