About Md Shohug Hossain

Ph.D. Researcher · GeoAI · Remote Sensing · Planetary Science

Professional Overview

Md Shohug Hossain is a doctoral fellow in Earth & Energy Sciences at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. His research bridges machine learning, planetary geology, and satellite remote sensing, with a focus on developing GeoAI tools for planetary surface analysis. His interdisciplinary expertise spans computer vision, deep learning, and geospatial analytics.

His research philosophy emphasizes “science through computation” — merging physics-based understanding with intelligent data modeling to decode natural processes on Earth and Mars.

Research Philosophy

Shohug’s work integrates AI and geoscience to uncover complex environmental and planetary patterns, emphasizing reproducibility, interdisciplinary collaboration, and data ethics.

Planetary Geomorphology

Exploring Mars’ surface evolution using geomorphic feature extraction and CTX/HiRISE imagery.

Deep Learning Models

Building AI pipelines for crater, mound, and landform detection across planetary environments.

Earth Observation

Applying satellite imagery and GeoAI to monitor climate-driven terrestrial transformations.
