About Me

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette specializing in GeoAI, Remote Sensing, and Planetary Science. My research explores how artificial intelligence can decode geomorphic and environmental processes on Earth and Mars through high-resolution imagery and data analytics.

I focus on building deep-learning frameworks for automatic landform detection, geomorphic classification, and planetary terrain mapping using satellite imagery from missions such as CTX, HiRISE, and Sentinel. My goal is to advance our understanding of landscape evolution while contributing to climate, hazard, and planetary exploration research.

Before joining UL Lafayette, I completed my M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Calgary and a B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering at North South University. My academic path bridges software systems, computer vision, and data-driven geoscience, allowing me to translate large datasets into actionable insights.
