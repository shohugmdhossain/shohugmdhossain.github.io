About Md Shohug Hossain

Md Shohug Hossain is a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette specializing in GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary science. His research explores how machine learning and geospatial analytics can decode geomorphological evolution on Earth and Mars.

He is passionate about developing AI-driven methods that transform planetary imagery into scientific understanding — bridging computation, geology, and environmental insight.

Academic & Research Journey

Ph.D. — University of Louisiana at Lafayette (2025–Present)

Doctoral research in GeoAI and planetary geomorphology using deep learning and high-resolution satellite data.

M.Sc. — University of Calgary (2023)

Master’s research on AI-based satellite image analysis and computer vision for geoscientific applications.

B.Sc. — North South University (2019)

Computer Science & Engineering major with focus on data analysis and image processing; awarded merit scholarship.

Research Interests

Planetary Geomorphology

Investigating surface processes and landform evolution on Mars and Earth using satellite imagery and deep learning.

Earth Observation

Developing geospatial AI models to monitor environmental dynamics and surface changes through remote sensing data.

Deep Learning & Climate Applications

Exploring intelligent systems for analyzing climate-driven transformations across terrestrial and planetary environments.

Beyond Research

Outside academia, Shohug enjoys photography, geological exploration, and astronomy. He mentors student researchers and promotes interdisciplinary education that unites science, art, and technology. His curiosity for both space and Earth continues to inspire innovative projects bridging AI and environmental discovery.
