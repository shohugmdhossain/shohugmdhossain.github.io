About Me

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette specializing in GeoAI, Remote Sensing, and Planetary Science. My research focuses on integrating deep learning with satellite and planetary imagery to study geomorphic and environmental processes across Earth and Mars.

Before joining UL Lafayette, I completed my M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Calgary, where I worked on intelligent automation and geospatial data systems. I also hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from North South University, Bangladesh.

My interdisciplinary background bridges computer vision, geoinformatics, and planetary geomorphology—allowing me to apply advanced AI models to understand planetary surfaces and Earth systems.

Research Interests

  • Planetary geomorphology and surface evolution modeling
  • Deep learning for remote sensing and environmental mapping
  • Multispectral and radar-based Earth observation
  • GeoAI for urban and climate applications

Beyond Research

Outside of research, I’m passionate about science communication, open-source collaboration, and mentoring students in data science and AI for sustainability. I also enjoy creating educational web tools and contributing to community-driven projects in geospatial analytics.
