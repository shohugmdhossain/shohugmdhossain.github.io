ABOUT ME

Ph.D. Researcher in GeoAI, Remote Sensing & Planetary Science

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette in the Earth & Energy Sciences program. My research integrates Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis to investigate both terrestrial and Martian landscapes. I use deep learning models such as Faster R-CNN and YOLO for feature detection and landscape interpretation from satellite imagery on HPC GPU systems.

My goal is to build scalable GeoAI pipelines that improve the accuracy and interpretability of surface mapping, enabling scientists to better understand Earth’s environmental changes and planetary evolution. I have experience in multispectral/hyperspectral data analysis, image segmentation, object detection, and geospatial data processing using Python, TensorFlow, and PyTorch.

Research Interests

  • Urban analytics and infrastructure mapping using GeoAI
  • Physics-informed neural networks and model interpretability

Beyond Research

Outside of research, I enjoy contributing to open-source projects, mentoring students, and collaborating on interdisciplinary problems that link computer vision, environmental science, and space exploration. My long-term vision is to advance explainable and transferable GeoAI systems that connect planetary and Earth observation for sustainable exploration and decision-making.
