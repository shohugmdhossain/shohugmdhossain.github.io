About Me

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, where I study GeoAI, Remote Sensing, and Planetary Science. My research merges machine learning and satellite imagery to understand geomorphic processes across Earth and Mars. I specialize in developing AI systems that automatically detect and classify surface landforms using optical and radar data.

My work involves large-scale image processing, deep-learning architecture optimization, and data integration across multi-mission sensors such as CTX, HiRISE, Sentinel, and Landsat. Through high-performance computing environments (HPC), I conduct experiments that enhance model reproducibility and accelerate inference pipelines for planetary terrain analysis.

I hold an M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering from the University of Calgary, where my graduate work focused on intelligent automation and data-driven systems. Earlier, I completed a B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering from North South University, where I first developed my interest in computer vision, AI, and environmental modeling.

My current academic interests span planetary geomorphology, GeoAI, and Earth observation systems. Beyond scientific research, I am passionate about interdisciplinary collaboration, open-source data, and teaching advanced AI tools for Earth and planetary science.

Beyond Research

Outside academia, I’m passionate about science outreach, leadership, and creative design. I also enjoy developing academic websites, visualizing data creatively, and mentoring early-career researchers. My approach blends analytical depth with design thinking — aiming to make complex ideas accessible and visually engaging.
