About Me

Md Shohug Hossain is a Ph.D. researcher specializing in GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary science.

Ph.D. Researcher, Department of Earth and Energy Sciences · University of Louisiana at Lafayette, USA

My academic focus integrates artificial intelligence with Earth and planetary data analysis. I develop machine-learning frameworks that interpret satellite imagery to understand geomorphic and environmental processes on Earth and Mars. My broader goal is to advance interpretable, physics-informed GeoAI methods that contribute to planetary exploration, hazard assessment, and sustainable Earth observation.

Research Interests

  • Planetary geomorphology and surface-process modeling
  • Deep learning for planetary and terrestrial landform detection
  • Remote sensing and data fusion across radar, optical, and hyperspectral domains
  • GeoAI for environmental monitoring and climate resilience

Technical Skills

  • Programming & AI: Python, PyTorch, Detectron2, YOLOv8, Scikit-learn
  • Geospatial Tools: ArcGIS, QGIS, Google Earth Engine, PostGIS
  • Data & Visualization: NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Remote Sensing: Multispectral & Hyperspectral image processing, CRISM, Sentinel, Landsat
  • Systems: Linux, Slurm, HPC computing environments
  • Web Development: HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Academic Journey

  • Ph.D. in Earth & Energy Sciences — University of Louisiana at Lafayette
    Focus: Planetary geomorphology, AI-driven terrain analysis, and remote sensing.
  • M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering — University of Calgary
    Focus: Intelligent automation and geospatial analytics.
  • B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering — North South University
    Focus: Software systems, computer vision, and data science foundations.

Beyond Research

Outside my research work, I enjoy contributing to academic leadership, creative visualization, and outreach. At the University of Calgary, I served as President of the ECE Graduate Group and Vice-President of the Schulich Graduate Engineering Consortium, promoting collaboration and student mentorship. I’m passionate about academic website design, science communication, and exploring how technology and art can make complex ideas more accessible.
