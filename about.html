About Me

My academic journey began with a fascination for computing and problem-solving during my undergraduate studies in Computer Science and Engineering at North South University, Bangladesh. I later pursued a Master’s in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Calgary, Canada, where I explored the integration of data-driven techniques with environmental and geospatial systems.

Currently, I am a Ph.D. Researcher in Earth and Energy Sciences at the University of Calgary. My research focuses on GeoAI-driven detection and classification of geomorphological features on Mars using high-resolution satellite imagery and high-performance computing (HPC) systems.

Research Focus

My research lies at the intersection of remote sensing, geospatial data science, and artificial intelligence. I develop computer vision models and scalable workflows to extract meaningful environmental information from satellite imagery. Beyond planetary mapping, I am broadly interested in Earth observation for sustainability — leveraging GeoAI to monitor wetlands, urban infrastructure, and environmental change.

Technical Expertise

I have hands-on experience with Detectron2, YOLO, Faster R-CNN, PyTorch, and geospatial tools such as ArcGIS and QGIS. My workflow integrates HPC environments (LONI and cloud systems for large-scale processing. I also use SHAP-based interpretability methods to analyze and explain GeoAI models.

Vision

I aim to bridge planetary and Earth science through intelligent geospatial modeling — designing data-driven tools that enhance our understanding of surface processes, climate change, and sustainable land use. My broader vision is to enable GeoAI applications that support both planetary exploration and climate resilience on Earth.

Beyond Research

Beyond my academic work, I enjoy creating visual stories with data, following space exploration missions, and contributing to open-source GeoAI projects. I’m also passionate about scientific communication — making complex spatial ideas accessible through clear visual and computational storytelling.
