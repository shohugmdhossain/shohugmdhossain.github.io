About Me

I am Md Shohug Hossain, a Ph.D. researcher at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette focusing on GeoAI, Remote Sensing, and Planetary Science. My research integrates machine learning and satellite imagery to investigate landscape evolution both on Earth and Mars. I’m deeply interested in developing explainable deep-learning models that uncover how geomorphic and environmental processes shape planetary terrains.

My academic journey spans multiple disciplines—from computer engineering and data science to planetary geomorphology. I’ve designed detection frameworks for surface features like craters and mounds, created public GeoAI datasets, and optimized deep learning workflows on HPC systems. My broader vision is to harness computational intelligence to better understand the changing surfaces of Earth and other planets.

Before joining UL Lafayette, I completed my M.Sc. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Calgary and a B.Sc. in Computer Science & Engineering at North South University. These experiences trained me in system design, image analysis, and software development—skills that now underpin my work in planetary GeoAI.

I have published, presented, and collaborated across international research groups, contributing to interdisciplinary studies that merge AI, geoscience, and environmental analytics. Beyond my academic life, I am passionate about science outreach, academic leadership, and mentoring emerging researchers who wish to bridge computing and Earth science.

Beyond Research

Outside my research, I’ve served in several leadership roles including President of the ECE Graduate Group and Vice-President of the Schulich Graduate Engineering Consortium at the University of Calgary. I actively mentor graduate students and advocate for collaborative science through open data and open-source platforms.

I also enjoy academic web design, science communication, and creating visually engaging educational content to simplify complex scientific ideas. My current interests extend into sustainable computing and planetary visualization using creative coding.
