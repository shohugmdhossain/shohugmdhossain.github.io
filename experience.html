Present Projects

Automated Mound Detection on Mars
University of Louisiana at Lafayette · 2025–Present

Developing deep-learning frameworks for automatic detection and classification of mound-like geomorphologies using high-resolution CTX and HiRISE imagery.

  • Implemented Faster R-CNN and Detectron2 models trained on COCO datasets with GPU acceleration on LONI HPC clusters.
  • Generated precision–recall evaluations, confusion matrices, and visualizations for model interpretability.
  • Refined dataset registration and labeling for high accuracy across complex terrain environments.

Spectral Unmixing of CRISM Imagery Using Autoencoders
Independent Project · 2025

Exploring nonlinear unmixing of hyperspectral data from CRISM imagery for mineralogical mapping and geochemical inference.

  • Developed deep autoencoder models for endmember extraction and abundance mapping of Martian surface compositions.
  • Validated performance against classical linear unmixing approaches using synthetic CRISM datasets.
  • Integrated topographic context to interpret alteration sequences and mineralogical evolution.
