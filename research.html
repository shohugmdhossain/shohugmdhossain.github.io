RESEARCH

Exploring GeoAI for Planetary and Earth Systems

I build scalable computer-vision pipelines for multi-sensor remote sensing (optical, SAR, thermal/CRISM) across Earth and Mars. My work combines deep learning, geospatial analysis, and HPC to extract structure, change, and process-level insights from imagery.

CURRENT PROJECTS

Active Research

2025—Planetary GeoAI

Mars Mounds Detection with Faster/Mask R-CNN (CTX)

Detecting & classifying mound-like geomorphic features in CTX imagery using Detectron2. Focus on scalable mapping within craters and morphological validation.

  • COCO annotations with custom JSON cleaners
  • Training/eval on LONI A100 80GB GPU nodes
  • Per-feature metrics & label-quality reviews
Methods Outputs
2025—Multi-sensor

Planetary Surface Evolution via Multi-Modal GeoAI

Integrating optical (CTX/HiRISE), radar (SAR), and thermal to infer surface dynamics and terrain evolution under varying illumination and environmental conditions.

  • Physics-informed priors for stability & trust
  • XAI to align predictions with geology
Overview
2025—Spectral

CRISM-Guided Mineral Mapping (inspired by JHU APL CRISM workflows)

Using CRISM spectral indices and lightweight unmixing to flag alteration signatures co-located with geomorphic contexts (e.g., crater rims, fans), then fusing with high-res texture cues.

  • Spectral indices → semantic priors for CV models
  • Joint spatial–spectral candidates for follow-up

2023–2024Change Detection

A ML Approach to Change Detection in Bangladesh Using Remote Sensing

Supervised and semi-supervised pipelines (Landsat/Sentinel) to map land-cover transitions in deltaic and urbanizing regions; harmonized time-series and bias-aware validation.

  • Cloud/shadow masking & reflectance harmonization
  • Post-classification refinement & uncertainty maps
Report
2022–2023EO Wetlands

Wetland Dynamics from Multi-Sensor Fusion

Combined SAR backscatter, NDWI, and seasonal composites to track hydroperiod and edge shifts in vegetated wetlands; lightweight CNNs for surface-state classification.

  • Seasonal phenology features for robustness
  • Cross-site generalization tests
2021–2022Urban EO

Urban Surface Condition Mapping from Multiresolution Imagery

Mapped surface types and degradation indicators by fusing medium-res satellite imagery with sparse high-res tiles; transfer-learning to reduce label costs.

  • Tile-level domain adaptation
  • Confidence-weighted ensembling
