Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), remote sensing, and planetary science. I develop interpretable deep-learning models to analyze multi-sensor satellite imagery for understanding geomorphic and environmental processes. By integrating physical insights with AI-based feature extraction, my work bridges data-driven and physics-based approaches to advance planetary surface analysis and Earth observation.

Current Research Projects

Mars Mound Detection Using Faster R-CNN

This project applies Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN frameworks to detect and classify mound-like structures on the Martian surface using CTX (Context Camera) imagery. The study leverages LONI A100 GPU clusters for high-resolution training and aims to improve morphological mapping accuracy across Martian craters.

Read more →

CRISM-Based Mineralogy Mapping (JHU/APL Collaboration)

Conducted with scientists at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory, this work utilizes CRISM spectral data to map surface mineralogy and compositional diversity across Mars. Deep feature learning and spectral unmixing methods are combined to interpret geochemical signatures and infer past aqueous environments.

Past Research Projects

Machine Learning for Land-Cover Change Detection in Bangladesh

Designed a change detection pipeline integrating Landsat 8 and Sentinel-2 imagery to monitor land-cover dynamics in Bangladesh. Applied Random Forest and XGBoost classifiers with NDVI-based spectral differencing to evaluate urban expansion and vegetation loss.

Read more →

Wetland Hydrodynamics and Vegetation Mapping

Used Sentinel-1 SAR and MODIS optical time series to track wetland water dynamics, vegetation stress, and phenology. The study demonstrated the synergy of radar and optical sensors in monitoring coastal ecosystem resilience.

Read more →

Urban Reflectance and Thermal Variability Modeling

Investigated surface temperature patterns in urban microclimates using Landsat TIR and MODIS emissivity bands. Findings supported improvements in urban heat island modeling and land-surface emissivity correction techniques.
