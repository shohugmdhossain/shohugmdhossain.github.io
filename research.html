RESEARCH

Exploring GeoAI for Planetary and Earth Systems

My research focuses on developing intelligent computer vision models for analyzing remote sensing imagery of both Earth and planetary surfaces. I work at the intersection of machine learning, geospatial analysis, and high-performance computing (HPC) to extract meaningful patterns from satellite data. The overarching goal is to design scalable GeoAI workflows that enhance our understanding of geomorphological processes and environmental change.

Planetary GeoAI — Mars Mounds Detection

I develop Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN models using Detectron2 to identify and classify mound-like features on the Martian surface using CTX (Context Camera) imagery. These features offer clues about sedimentary, volcanic, or erosional processes on Mars. The project combines AI-based object detection with geomorphological validation to create one of the first scalable mapping frameworks for mound detection in Martian craters.

  • Dataset preprocessing using COCO-style annotations via Roboflow and custom JSON correction scripts.
  • Model training and evaluation on LONI HPC GPU nodes (A100 80GB).
  • Integration of per-feature performance metrics and confusion matrices for label consistency analysis.

Planetary Surface Evolution Modeling

This research focuses on integrating multi-sensor datasets (optical, radar, and thermal) to understand geomorphic evolution and temporal surface changes on Mars and other planetary bodies. Using a multi-modal GeoAI framework, we aim to infer surface dynamics and classify terrain changes under varying illumination and environmental conditions.

  • Combining optical CTX, HiRISE, and thermal infrared data for temporal analysis.
  • Developing physics-informed models to link morphological features with environmental variables.
  • Exploring explainable AI to interpret model predictions in the context of planetary geology.

Earth Observation & Urban GeoAI

On the Earth side, I focus on geospatial AI for infrastructure monitoring, walkability analysis, and wetland change detection. Using multi-resolution satellite and street-level imagery, I design automated workflows to extract urban features and evaluate accessibility and sustainability indicators.

  • Developing a sidewalk and curb-ramp inventory system for urban accessibility mapping using computer vision.
  • Applying deep learning to classify surface conditions and detect infrastructure gaps.
  • Integrating model performance validation through automated label review pipelines.

Research in Progress

  • Cross-domain GeoAI for transferable learning between Earth and planetary datasets.
  • Integrating climate variables with remote sensing for interpretable AI-driven hazard prediction.
  • Collaborative planetary mapping for open-access scientific reproducibility.
