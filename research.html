Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis, integrating artificial intelligence with physical reasoning to uncover geomorphic and environmental processes across Earth and Mars. I explore how machine learning, data fusion, and geoscientific insight can jointly advance our understanding of planetary evolution, urban accessibility, and sustainable landscapes.

Planetary GeoAI

This theme investigates how deep learning and computer vision can detect, classify, and interpret geomorphic features on Mars. I use orbital datasets to study mound and crater formations, aiming to reveal clues about sedimentary processes and paleoenvironments that shaped the planet’s surface.

Earth Observation & Accessibility

I apply AI-based geospatial analytics to assess walkability and urban resilience. By mapping sidewalks, ramps, and crosswalks from high-resolution imagery, this work contributes to data-driven accessibility, inclusive design, and sustainable infrastructure planning.

Physics-Informed ML & Data Fusion

My current work explores embedding physical constraints into AI models and fusing optical, radar, and thermal observations to build interpretable, trustworthy, and scalable GeoAI systems for both planetary and Earth applications.

Recent Highlights

Automated Detection of Martian Mounds

Developed deep-learning pipelines using high-resolution CTX imagery to map Martian mound-like features, improving understanding of surface processes and sedimentary history.

Urban Walkability Assessment

Led AI-driven analysis of sidewalk accessibility using aerial and street-level imagery, producing validated per-feature accuracy metrics to support inclusive city planning.

Physics-Informed GeoAI on HPC

Integrated physics-guided constraints into GeoAI models and deployed multi-sensor data fusion experiments on high-performance computing systems to enhance interpretability and scalability.
