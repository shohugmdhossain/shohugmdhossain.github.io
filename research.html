Projects

Research

Planetary GeoAI — Mars Mounds (CTX)

Developing deep-learning models using Detectron2 and LONI A100 GPUs to automatically detect and classify mound-like features on Mars from CTX imagery. The project applies Faster/Mask R-CNN with COCO-format datasets and topographic fusion to improve detection under challenging illumination and terrain.

The goal is to infer geological processes—such as past hydrological or volcanic activity—through large-scale morphological mapping, contributing to planetary stratigraphy and site selection for future Mars missions.

Earth Observation — Wetland Change Detection

Applying multispectral and hyperspectral imagery (Sentinel-2, Landsat-9, ECOSTRESS) to identify wetland disturbance and resilience. Uses spectral indices (NDVI, NDWI, EVI) and temporal ML models such as Random Forest and LSTM to track ecosystem recovery patterns over time.

The study supports conservation policy and land-use planning by delivering scalable, AI-driven pipelines for wetland health monitoring.

Urban GeoAI — Sidewalk Inventory & Accessibility Mapping

Designing a computer-vision workflow to map sidewalk elements—curb ramps, crosswalks, buffers, surfaces—from street-level imagery. An automated label-auditing system detects annotation errors and improves per-feature F1-scores through re-training and confusion-matrix evaluation.

The results strengthen municipal GIS datasets and empower accessibility planning by producing interpretable, reproducible infrastructure analytics.

Research in Progress

  • Expanding wetland monitoring with long-term time-series data and ecosystem resilience metrics.
  • Developing GeoAI-based walkability prediction models from panoramic street-view imagery.
