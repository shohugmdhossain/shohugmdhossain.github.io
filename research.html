Research Overview

My research integrates Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), remote sensing, and planetary science. I build interpretable deep-learning models and scalable workflows to analyze geomorphology, mineralogy, and urban infrastructure across both Earth and Mars. My work combines AI-driven automation with physics-informed reasoning to enhance scientific discovery.

Current Projects (2024–2025)

Mars Mound Detection Using Deep Learning

Planetary Geomorphology · 2024–Present
Objective: Automate detection of mound-like geological structures in CTX imagery to improve geomorphic mapping of Martian craters.

Outcome: Developed a high-accuracy Faster R-CNN model (mAP 0.83) trained on 5,000+ labeled regions.
Description: Leveraging LONI A100 GPUs for large-scale training and validation. Integrated COCO JSON annotation workflows and Detectron2-based segmentation pipelines.

CRISM Spectral Mineral Mapping

Planetary Spectroscopy · 2024–Present
Objective: Map surface mineral composition and alteration signatures on Mars using CRISM hyperspectral data.
Description: Designed an autoencoder-based deep unmixing model for high-dimensional spectra, supporting detection of hydrated minerals linked to ancient aqueous processes.
