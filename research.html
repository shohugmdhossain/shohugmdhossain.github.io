Research in GeoAI & Planetary Science

Exploring Earth and Mars using Artificial Intelligence, Remote Sensing, and Geospatial Analytics

Research Overview

My research integrates deep learning, physics-based modeling, and multi-sensor satellite data to understand geomorphological, environmental, and climatic processes across Earth and planetary systems.

Current Projects

Mars Mound Detection Using Deep Learning

Planetary Geomorphology · 2025

Developing an automated system to detect and classify mound-like geomorphological features on Mars using Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN. The workflow leverages LONI A100 GPUs to train models on high-resolution CTX imagery, improving our understanding of crater-related landforms.

This project bridges machine learning with planetary science by automating the mapping of mounds that may indicate volcanic or sedimentary origins — offering new insights into Mars' geologic past.

PyTorch Detectron2 Planetary Science GPU Computing

CRISM Spectral Mineral Mapping (JHU/APL Collaboration)

Hyperspectral Analysis · 2025

Working with scientists at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory to develop deep-learning spectral unmixing pipelines for CRISM hyperspectral data. These models extract mineralogical compositions, revealing the presence of aqueous alteration minerals across Martian terrains.

The study integrates convolutional feature learning with physical reflectance modeling to interpret ancient hydrological activity on Mars.

CRISM Spectral Python Autoencoder Hyperspectral Imaging

Wetland Hydrodynamics and Vegetation Monitoring

Ecosystem Dynamics · 2023

Used Sentinel-1 SAR and MODIS optical data to model hydrological cycles, vegetation seasonality, and floodplain dynamics. The integration of radar and optical sensors enables high temporal resolution mapping of wetland health and ecosystem resilience.

Sentinel-1 MODIS Google Earth Engine SAR Analytics

Urban GeoAI for Accessibility Mapping

Computer Vision · 2024

Created an AI-powered sidewalk element inventory system using YOLOv8 and Faster R-CNN to detect sidewalk features (curb ramps, crosswalks, buffers) from street-level imagery. Post-validation workflows integrate confusion matrices for per-feature accuracy analysis.

YOLOv8 Computer Vision Geospatial AI Model Validation

Land-Cover Change Detection in Bangladesh

Remote Sensing · 2022

Developed a change-detection workflow using Landsat 8 and Sentinel-2 imagery to track urban growth and vegetation loss. The integration of XGBoost and Random Forest classifiers with NDVI differencing achieved over 90% classification accuracy.
