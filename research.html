Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of planetary geomorphology, remote sensing, and artificial intelligence. I develop data-driven frameworks that apply deep learning and physics-informed modeling to understand surface evolution on Earth and Mars. By integrating multisensor satellite data with high-performance computing, my work aims to advance reproducible GeoAI methods for terrain interpretation, environmental monitoring, and planetary exploration.

Research Themes

Planetary Geomorphology

Study of Martian and terrestrial surface processes through AI-based terrain analysis, quantifying landform evolution and morphodynamics from orbital imagery.

GeoAI & Deep Learning

Designing neural frameworks for automatic landform detection, semantic segmentation, and geomorphic classification across planetary datasets.

Remote Sensing Integration

Fusing radar, optical, and hyperspectral observations to study surface composition, hydrological dynamics, and environmental change.

Physics-Informed Modeling

Developing interpretable AI models anchored in geophysical principles to enhance scientific transparency and trust in GeoAI applications.

Present Projects

Automated Mound Detection on Mars
University of Louisiana at Lafayette · 2025–Present

Developing deep-learning frameworks for detection and classification of mound-like geomorphologies using CTX and HiRISE imagery.

  • Implemented Faster R-CNN models in Detectron2 on LONI HPC systems for large-scale planetary surface analysis.
  • Generated precision–recall evaluations and interpretability maps for geomorphic feature detection.
  • Optimized dataset alignment and augmentation to improve detection consistency across Martian basins.

Spectral Unmixing of CRISM Imagery Using Autoencoders
Independent Project · 2025

Investigating nonlinear spectral unmixing of CRISM hyperspectral imagery for mineralogical mapping and alteration history of Martian terrains.

  • Developed deep autoencoder models for endmember extraction and abundance estimation.
  • Compared deep-learning results with linear models to evaluate spectral interpretability.
  • Linked mineralogical results with geomorphic context for surface-process inference.
