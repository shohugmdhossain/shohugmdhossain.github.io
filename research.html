RESEARCH

Exploring GeoAI for Planetary and Earth Systems

My research focuses on developing intelligent computer vision models for analyzing remote sensing imagery of both Earth and planetary surfaces. I work at the intersection of machine learning, geospatial analysis, and high-performance computing (HPC) to extract meaningful patterns from satellite data. The overarching goal is to design scalable GeoAI workflows that enhance our understanding of geomorphological processes and environmental change.

Current Projects

Planetary GeoAI — Mars Mound Detection

Developing Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN models with Detectron2 to identify mound-like geomorphic features in CTX imagery of Martian craters. These features provide insight into sedimentary and erosional processes.

  • COCO-style dataset curation and JSON validation pipeline.
  • Training on LONI QB-3/QB-4 HPC GPUs (A100 80 GB nodes).
  • Per-feature confusion matrix analysis for label reliability.

CRISM-based Spectral Modeling (Johns Hopkins APL)

Integrating CRISM hyperspectral data with CNN-based models for mapping hydrated mineral deposits across Mars. This collaboration with the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory focuses on spectral feature extraction and semi-supervised learning for compositional analysis.

  • Spectral unmixing and PCA-based dimensionality reduction.
  • CNN encoder for cross-sensor spectral transfer learning.
  • Validation with MRO and HiRISE contextual datasets.

Past Projects

A Machine Learning Approach to Change Detection in Bangladesh

Applied remote sensing and machine learning techniques to detect urban expansion and vegetation loss across Bangladesh using Landsat 8 imagery. The study integrated NDVI and built-up indices with classification models to quantify land-use transitions between 2000–2020.

  • Used Random Forest and SVM classifiers for multi-temporal image analysis.
  • Processed and harmonized Landsat & Sentinel-2 datasets using Google Earth Engine.
  • Generated regional urban growth maps validated against census data.

Wetland Change Detection Using Multi-temporal Remote Sensing

Analyzed changes in wetland distribution and waterbody dynamics using NDWI and SAR backscatter data. Integrated optical and radar sources to improve seasonal consistency and cloud resilience in classification.

  • Multi-source fusion of Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 imagery.
  • Normalized difference and threshold-based wetland delineation.
  • Temporal visualization of shrinkage and hydrological fluctuation trends.

Surface Temperature Estimation via Thermal Infrared Analysis

Developed a pipeline for retrieving land surface temperature (LST) from MODIS and Landsat thermal bands. Modeled temporal trends in temperature anomalies and correlated them with vegetation cover indices for environmental monitoring.

  • Atmospheric correction and emissivity calibration workflow.
  • Python-based LST retrieval and heat island mapping.
  • Visualization of annual change trends using ArcGIS Pro.
