Research Overview

My research focuses on Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis. I develop interpretable AI systems to process multisensor satellite imagery for understanding geomorphic, climatic, and urban processes across Earth and Mars. My work integrates physics-informed modeling, machine learning, and high-performance computing to enable more reliable geospatial interpretations.

Current & Ongoing Projects

Mars Mound Detection Using Deep Learning

Planetary Geomorphology · 2024–Present

Detecting and classifying mound-like structures on Mars using Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN architectures trained on CTX imagery. Leveraging LONI A100 GPU clusters for large-scale morphological mapping and validation.

Faster R-CNN Detectron2 COCO JSON GPU (A100)

CRISM Spectral Mineral Mapping

Planetary Spectroscopy · 2024–Present

Mapping surface mineral diversity on Mars through CRISM hyperspectral data. Implementing autoencoder-based spectral unmixing to identify hydrated mineral signatures and reconstruct past aqueous processes.

CRISM Autoencoders Spectral Python (SPy) Deep Unmixing

Urban Accessibility Mapping with AI

Computer Vision · 2024

Developing a vision-based GeoAI pipeline to detect sidewalk elements — curb ramps, crosswalks, and traffic controls — for urban accessibility assessment. Introduced LLM-assisted label auditing to refine ground truth data and enhance detection reliability.
