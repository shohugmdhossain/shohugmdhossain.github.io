Research in GeoAI & Planetary Science

Integrating artificial intelligence, remote sensing, and planetary data to explore Earth and beyond.

Research Overview

My research focuses on combining data-driven deep learning and physically informed models to interpret multi-sensor satellite imagery for both Earth and planetary environments.

Current Research

Mars Mound Detection Using Deep Learning

Planetary Geomorphology · 2025

This project applies Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN to detect mound-like structures across Martian CTX imagery. Leveraging LONI A100 GPU clusters, the study refines detection pipelines and accuracy assessments to automate crater-related geomorphological analysis.

Results aim to improve automated planetary surface mapping and contribute to understanding ancient aqueous and volcanic processes that shaped Martian landscapes.

PyTorch Detectron2 GPU Computing Planetary Imagery

CRISM Spectral Mineral Mapping

Spectroscopy · JHU/APL Collaboration

This project uses CRISM hyperspectral data to map surface mineralogy across Mars. Combining deep spectral unmixing and autoencoder networks, the model identifies compositional transitions that signal aqueous alteration zones.

The findings bridge geomorphological observations and geochemical processes, enhancing planetary mineral exploration workflows.

Past Research

Land-Cover Change Detection Using Machine Learning

Remote Sensing · Bangladesh · 2022–2023

Designed a supervised classification system using Landsat 8 and Sentinel-2 imagery to detect urban expansion and vegetation loss. The workflow integrates Random Forest and XGBoost models, achieving high accuracy for spatiotemporal change mapping.

Landsat 8 Sentinel-2 XGBoost Remote Sensing

Wetland Hydrodynamics and Vegetation Monitoring

Ecosystem Dynamics · 2019–2021

Analyzed Sentinel-1 SAR and MODIS optical time series to model hydrodynamic behavior and phenological cycles in wetlands. This study demonstrated how radar–optical synergy captures vegetation stress and seasonal flooding with high temporal fidelity.

Sentinel-1 MODIS Time Series Analysis Wetland Ecology

Urban Reflectance and Thermal Variability Modeling

Urban Climate · 2020

Investigated surface emissivity and thermal heterogeneity in dense urban areas using Landsat TIR and MODIS LST products. The study provided insights into microclimate variability and improved land-surface temperature correction methods for urban heat analysis.
