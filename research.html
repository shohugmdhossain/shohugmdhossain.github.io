Research Overview

My research integrates Geospatial Artificial Intelligence (GeoAI), remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis to investigate both terrestrial and extraterrestrial landscapes. I combine machine learning, image processing, and high-performance computing to design interpretable and scalable models that extract meaningful patterns from satellite and spectral imagery.

Current Research Projects

Planetary GeoAI — Mars Mound Detection

Developing Faster R-CNN and Mask R-CNN models within the Detectron2 framework for detecting mound-like features on Mars using CTX imagery. These geomorphological features provide insight into sedimentary and volcanic processes shaping the Martian surface.

  • Automated COCO-format labeling and JSON validation for large-scale CTX datasets.
  • Model training on LONI QB-3/QB-4 A100 GPUs with multi-class metrics.
  • Integrated confusion-matrix validation for class-level consistency.

CRISM-Based Spectral Feature Analysis (with JHU Applied Physics Lab)

Collaborating with Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory on hyperspectral data exploration using CRISM (Compact Reconnaissance Imaging Spectrometer for Mars) datasets to characterize mineralogical variations in mound-bearing terrains.

  • Processing hyperspectral CRISM cubes for compositional unmixing and feature extraction.
  • Integration with high-resolution CTX and HiRISE data for contextual interpretation.
  • Developing spectral clustering and PCA-based mineral classification workflows.

Earth Observation & Urban GeoAI

Designing AI-powered geospatial systems to map urban accessibility, infrastructure conditions, and environmental patterns using satellite and street-level imagery. This research connects urban science with sustainability and inclusive design.

  • Developed a sidewalk and curb-ramp inventory for urban accessibility mapping.
  • Created GeoAI pipelines integrating TensorFlow, PyTorch, and OpenCV.
  • Validated model performance through per-class F1 metrics and automated label reviews.
Past Projects & Explorations

A Machine Learning Approach to Change Detection in Bangladesh

Conducted spatio-temporal land cover change analysis using Landsat 8 and Sentinel-2 imagery to quantify urban expansion and vegetation loss in coastal regions of Bangladesh. Employed machine learning-based classification and spectral differencing to track long-term transformations.

  • Used Random Forest and SVM classifiers for multi-year change mapping.
  • Developed NDVI and NDBI-based indices to analyze vegetation–urban transitions.

Wetland and Ecosystem Mapping using Multispectral Imagery

Applied multispectral and radar-based remote sensing to identify wetland boundaries and vegetation dynamics across monsoon cycles. This study demonstrated the potential of Sentinel-1 SAR and Landsat 8 OLI fusion for ecosystem monitoring.

  • Developed spectral threshold models for seasonal wetland detection.
  • Generated vegetation dynamics maps for change trend visualization.

Foundational Work on Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Terrain Analysis

Early experiments applying physics-informed ML models to couple geomorphic parameters (slope, curvature, and albedo) with model-driven surface predictions. This line of work informs explainable GeoAI development for both Earth and Mars.
