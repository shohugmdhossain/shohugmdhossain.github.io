Research Overview

My research focuses on the intersection of GeoAI, remote sensing, and planetary surface analysis, integrating artificial intelligence with physical reasoning to uncover geomorphic and environmental processes across Earth and Mars. I explore how machine learning, data fusion, and geoscientific insight can jointly advance our understanding of planetary evolution, urban accessibility, and sustainable landscapes.

Planetary GeoAI

Deep learning and computer vision for detecting and interpreting geomorphic features on Mars from orbital datasets, with implications for sedimentary processes and paleoenvironments.

Earth Observation & Accessibility

AI-based geospatial analytics for walkability and urban resilience—mapping sidewalks, ramps, and crosswalks to inform inclusive design and sustainable infrastructure planning.

Physics-Informed ML & Data Fusion

Embedding physical constraints into models and fusing optical, radar, and thermal observations to build interpretable, trustworthy, and scalable GeoAI for Earth and planetary science.

Recent Highlights

Automated Detection of Martian Mounds

Developed deep-learning pipelines on CTX imagery to map mound-like features, improving understanding of surface processes and sedimentary history.

Urban Walkability Assessment

AI-driven analyses of sidewalk accessibility with validated per-feature metrics to support inclusive city planning.

Physics-Informed GeoAI on HPC

Deployed multi-sensor fusion with physics-guided constraints on high-performance systems to enhance interpretability and scale.
